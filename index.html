Research interests

Questions behind the projects.

Embodied & spatial interaction

Designing interactive installations and artifacts that respond to movement, light, and sound in physical spaces. Exploring how subtle changes in behavior and ambience shape people’s feelings in a place.

Creative tools & learning

How creative coding tools, tangible interfaces, and playful prototypes support exploration and learning for people who may not see themselves as “technical.”

Speculative & critical design

Using speculative interfaces and narratives to question how technologies mediate attention, care, and everyday routines.

Methods & evaluation

Combining prototyping, observational studies, and qualitative feedback to understand how people actually experience interactive work, and to iterate based on those encounters.

Process & methods

How projects move from idea to experience.

Exploration

Sketching, storyboarding, and quick digital or physical mockups to map ideas and questions. Collecting references from everyday life, installations, and media.

Prototyping

Building working prototypes using creative coding, Arduino, and simple fabrication. Iterating on form, motion, and interaction based on how the piece feels in context.

Evaluation

Informal user studies, observations, and short interviews to see how people actually engage. Adjusting details of timing, light, and feedback to better match the intended experience.

About Naz

Background, skills, and what she enjoys working on.

Naz Khani (Fatemeh Hadavandkhani) is a creative technologist and designer graduating from the MS in Creative Technology & Design at the University of Colorado Boulder. She is interested in how interactive systems and designed artifacts shape everyday experiences of light, space, play, and connection.

Her work often combines physical computing, visual design, and creative coding to build prototypes that can be experienced, not just imagined. She enjoys moving between code and craft — prototyping with Arduino one day, refining visual systems the next.
